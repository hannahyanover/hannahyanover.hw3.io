I aimed to create a campfire sound, which is a combination of a continuous hiss and irregular crackles. The hiss was first generated with white noise, passed through a high-pass filter (~1 kHz) and a gain node. This simulates the airy sound of the flame, creating a steady, constant texture.The crackles were made with short bursts of filtered noise using a bandpass filter (~3 kHz) and a fast gain envelope to create quick, popping sounds. Crackles are triggered randomly using setInterval() with some probability, producing irregular, realistic fire snaps. This sound uses subtractive synthesis for shaping the hiss and crackles, plus envelope shaping and random triggering to emulate the irregular behavior of a fire. The two components combine to create a campfire sound.
